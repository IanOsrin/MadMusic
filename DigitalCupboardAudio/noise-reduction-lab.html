3 · Reduction Parameters

Amplifies the noise region before FFT — reveals low-level spectral detail that would otherwise hide under quantisation/analysis floor. Scaled back down before matching against signal.
Minimum gain for noise-dominated bins. Counterintuitive: leaving more residual (smaller values) masks artifacts and sounds cleaner than deeper cuts.
Signal has to exceed (noise + this) to pass through untouched. Higher = more aggressive gate.
Multiplier on the noise estimate. 1.0 = literal, 1.5–2.0 = typical, higher = more aggressive (more musical-noise risk).
Bigger window = finer frequency resolution (better for steady hiss) but smears transients. 2048 is a good default.
Averages noise profile across neighbouring frequency bins. Reduces musical-noise birdies at the cost of some frequency precision.
Decision-directed prior SNR (Ephraim–Malah): blends current frame with previous-frame signal estimate. Higher = stronger temporal continuity, far less watery sound.
How slowly the per-bin gate closes after signal fades. Longer = smoother, no flicker. Attack is fixed fast (5 ms) to preserve transients.
